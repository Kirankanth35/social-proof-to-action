# Project Summary

## Title
**From Social Proof to Action: Algorithmic Amplification as a Conditional Mechanism with Reach Saturation and Authority Effects**

## Short description
This project examines how early persuasive signals, algorithmic visibility, influencer authority, and high-effort engagement relate in social media environments. The central argument is that visibility should not be treated as the same thing as meaningful engagement.

## Data
- 10,000 social media posts
- 800 unique influencers
- 35 variables in the main worksheet
- Date range: 2024-01-01 to 2025-12-30

## Main constructs
- **Persuasive power**
- **Algorithmic amplification**
- **Influencer authority**
- **High-effort engagement**

## Methods
- feature engineering
- descriptive statistics
- clustered OLS
- moderated mediation logic
- nonlinear reach modeling
- high-visibility interaction analysis

## Key findings
- Early persuasive signals are positively associated with amplification.
- Direct amplification to high-effort engagement is weak on average.
- Authority strengthens the pathway from early signals to meaningful action.
- Reach shows diminishing returns around log reach ≈ 14.18.
- Very high visibility weakens the predictive role of persuasive power.
